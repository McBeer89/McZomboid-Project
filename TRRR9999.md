## Technique Overview

This document outlines the community rules and guidelines for a Project Zomboid multiplayer server. These rules govern player interactions, loot management, vehicle usage, communication protocols, and fair play practices to maintain an immersive roleplay environment.

The primary objective is to ensure all players have a positive experience while maintaining the integrity of the survival simulation through structured social interaction mechanics.

## Technical Background

These are community governance rules that define:
- Expected behaviors for player interactions
- Mechanisms for conflict resolution (robbery, raiding)
- Fair play enforcement systems 
- Resource management protocols
- Roleplay requirements and limitations

The server operates on Project Zomboid's multiplayer framework with specific modifications to enforce these community guidelines.

## Procedures Section

### Procedure 1: Theft & Robbery Protocol
**ID**: TRR9999.ZOMBIOD.COMMUNITY.ROBBERY.A

This procedure defines the rules for player-to-player interactions involving theft:
- Ghost theft (sneaky stealing) is prohibited
- All theft must involve roleplay - visual and verbal acknowledgment required
- Compliance vs PvP outcomes are clearly defined with specific limitations on what can be taken
- The 5-second rule applies to initiate combat after RP declaration

### Procedure 2: Safehouse Raiding Protocol  
**ID**: TRR9999.ZOMBIOD.COMMUNITY.RAIDING.A

This procedure governs safehouse warfare mechanics:
- Online presence required for valid raids
- Roleplay initiation must occur before claiming war
- Two victory conditions (combat breach or surrender)
- Strict looting limitations to prevent inventory manipulation
- Post-raid release and cooldown requirements

### Procedure 3: Group Separation & Dissolution
**ID**: TRR9999.ZOMBIOD.COMMUNITY.DISSOLUTION.A

This procedure manages faction splits:
- No scorched earth policy for structures built during group existence
- Defined loot distribution rules (severance package)
- One trip rule prevents return trips after leaving
- In-character vs out-of-character behavior requirements

### Procedure 4: Character Creation Requirements
**ID**: TRR9999.ZOMBIOD.COMMUNITY.CHARACTER.A

This procedure governs character creation:
- Mandatory character biography submission required
- Realistic stat limits to prevent overpowered characters
- Narrative consistency requirements for traits and backstory
- Clothing and starting gear restrictions

### Procedure 5: World Container Integrity
**ID**: TRR9999.ZOMBIOD.COMMUNITY.CONTAINER.A

This procedure protects game world integrity:
- Prohibition on dismantling key containers in important locations
- Specific affected areas listed (police stations, hospitals, etc.)
- Clarifications on what is allowed (barricading, player-made containers)

### Procedure 6: Exploit Prevention
**ID**: TRR9999.ZOMBIOD.COMMUNITY.EXPLOIT.A

This procedure enforces fair play:
- Clear definition of game exploits
- Consequences for exploit use ranging from character remake to permanent ban
- Distinction between reporting bugs and abusing them

### Procedure 7: Vehicle Management
**ID**: TRR9999.ZOMBIOD.COMMUNITY.VEHICLE.A

This procedure governs vehicle usage:
- Hoarding guidelines for vehicles
- "Leave it Drivable" scrapping protocol
- Allowed part stripping rules

### Procedure 8: Communication Protocols  
**ID**: TRR9999.ZOMBIOD.COMMUNITY.COMMS.A

This procedure defines communication standards:
- Radio chat restrictions and usage
- Out-of-character chat requirements with proper formatting
