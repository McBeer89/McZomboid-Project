## 2. Theft & Robbery

We want to encourage interaction, even hostile interaction. You are a survivor, not an invisible ghost.

### A â€” No Ghost Theft

You cannot steal from another player without interacting with them first.

- No sneaking up on someone while they're looting or fishing to grab items from their pack or trunk without them noticing
- No hotwiring and driving off in a player's vehicle while they're nearby unless a proper robbery has been initiated

### B â€” Robbery Protocol *(The Highwayman Rule)*

To rob someone you must:

- **Reveal yourself** â€” be visually and verbally present. No robbing via chat while hiding in a bush, and no using camera angles to attack from above before RP begins
- **Issue a clear demand** â€” *"Hands up, drop the bag!"* or *"Step away from the car!"*

### C â€” Compliance vs. PvP

**If the victim complies** (hands up, stops moving):

- You **cannot kill them**
- **Fighting Chance Clause:** Leave the bare minimum to survive â€” don't take their last melee weapon, last bandage, food, water, clothing, or backpack
- **Loot OR Ride â€” pick one:**
  - Take their **gear** â†’ they keep the vehicle
  - Take their **vehicle** â†’ they keep their gear
  - You cannot take both

**If the victim resists or flees:**

- They consent to PvP and waive the protections above â€” if they lose, the aggressor may loot them fully
- **5-Second Rule:** Once PvP is agreed (can be confirmed OOC), a 5-second grace period must be observed so both sides can prepare

> **Note:** If the defender flees without any RP, the 5-second rule is void. If either side attacks or approaches aggressively without RP first, the other may freely defend themselves.

### D â€” Borrowing & Trading

Before going hostile, try talking. Many survivors will trade or give you a ride if you just ask. It's worth using Steam's recording tools or screenshots to document any major interactions.

## 3. Safehouse Wars (Raiding)

We use the **Claim War** mechanic to simulate raids. Abusing it to grief will get you banned.

### A â€” When a Raid is Valid

**Offline raiding is strictly forbidden.**

- **Solo player:** Their base may only be raided when *that player* is online
- **Faction (2+ members):** The base may only be raided when *over 50%* of active members are online and present at the location

### B â€” Initiation

- **RP first** â€” you cannot just click Claim War. Make yourself visually and verbally known, then give defenders **2 minutes** to react
- **Defenders must accept** â€” refusing the War Request to hide behind game protections is Fail RP

### C â€” Victory Conditions

**The Hard Way (Combat):** Attackers need **4 unique defender kills** to breach. Each kill damages safehouse protection; once the threshold is reached, containers unlock. Only one kill per defender counts toward the total â€” do not kill respawning defenders to pad the count. Fighting ends at breach â€” respawning defenders act as hostages until the scene wraps up.

**The Easy Way (Surrender):** Defenders temporarily add one attacker to the Safehouse Allow-list. Remove them once the looting limit is reached.

### D â€” Looting Limits *(Smash & Grab)*

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Designated Looter | **One person only** enters to loot â€” everyone else stays outside |
| Time Limit | **3 minutes** from the moment containers are accessible |
| Carry Limit | Personal carry only â€” inventory + backpacks + hands. No loading vehicles, no swaps, one trip |

A raid is chaotic and fast. You don't have time for inventory Tetris â€” grab what looks valuable and go.

### E â€” Post-Raid

- Leave immediately once loot is secured â€” you cannot occupy the base or keep the claim
- **7-day cooldown:** A group can only attack the same target once every 7 real-life days

## 4. Group Separation & Dissolution

Factions split. It happens. OOC ego does not get to destroy someone's gameplay experience on the way out.

### A â€” No Scorched Earth

Structures belong to the base, not the builder. If you leave or get kicked, you are **strictly forbidden** from dismantling walls, destroying containers, or burning anything down out of spite.

### B â€” The Severance Package

The leaving player is entitled to:

| Category | What You Can Take |
|---|---|
| Personal property | Full contents of your personal container or bedroom |
| Food & water | Up to 2 days' worth of rations *(if available)* |
| Medical | One standard first aid kit â€” bandages, disinfectant, painkillers *(if available)* |
| Defense | One melee weapon, one ranged weapon, 2 boxes of ammo *(if available)* |
| Tools | Flashlight, 1 extra battery, hammer, saw, up to 1 box of nails *(if available)* |
| Transport | One working vehicle â€” your original if you had one, otherwise a standard group vehicle |

> Don't try to stash communal loot in your personal container right before leaving to claim it.

### C â€” One Trip

Once you drive away, you're done. No coming back for a second load. Anything left behind belongs to the faction staying at the base.

### D â€” Keep It In-Character

If things get heated, handle it through RP â€” not OOC demands in Discord. Personal insults or harassment during a split will result in removal. If someone gets toxic, disengage and document it rather than matching their energy.

## 6. Realistic Character Builds

The extra starting points are there so you can build a *unique, fleshed-out survivor* â€” not a Super Soldier.

### A â€” No Min-Maxing

- **Sleep traits** (Sleepyhead, Restless Sleeper, etc.) are considered free point exploits since sleep is modified in multiplayer â€” don't take them
- Don't pile on extra negative traits purely to max out positives â€” that's the same kind of exploit in the other direction

### B â€” No Super Soldiers

- Don't create characters with maxed (9 or 10) **both** Strength and Fitness at the same time â€” it's not realistic
- A military background doesn't justify maxing both stats
- Want to be a powerhouse? **Earn it through in-game training**

### C â€” Narrative Consistency

- Your traits should make sense for your backstory â€” an IT worker probably shouldn't have Axeman and Hunter
- You don't have to obsessively roleplay every mechanical negative trait, but your RP can't flat-out contradict them (e.g. if you took Illiterate, you can't read maps in-character)

### D â€” Starting Gear & Clothing

All clothing options are unlocked for creative expression â€” not for mechanical advantage.

- Starting gear must match your bio
- Don't spawn in high-tier armor (firefighter gear, bulletproof vests) or military backpacks unless your bio clearly justifies it
- Admins can check character sheets and inventories â€” violations mean a character remake

## 7. Key Loot Locations â€” Don't Claim, Don't Strip

These locations exist for the whole server. Two rules apply to all of them:

1. **Don't claim them as a safehouse** â€” claiming a building stops loot from respawning inside it, cutting everyone else off from that resource permanently
2. **Don't remove or dismantle the containers** â€” loot spawns *inside* these containers; take the container and you delete future spawns for everyone

### Protected Locations

- Police Stations & Courthouses *(gun lockers, evidence lockers)*
- Fire Stations *(clothing lockers)*
- Hospitals & Clinics *(medical cabinets, shelving)*
- Military Installations *(barracks lockers, crates)*
- Gun Stores & Hunting Lodges *(display cases, metal shelves)*
- Large Warehouses & Factories *(industrial crates and shelving)*

### What IS Allowed

- **Barricading:** You can move a container *within the same building* to block a door
- **Loose loot containers:** Empty duffel bags and similar floor-loot items are fair game

> Take the supplies. Leave the shelves for the next person.

### Consequences

| Offense | Punishment |
|---|---|

### Bugs vs. Exploits

## 9. Vehicle Stewardship

Vehicles are finite. Look after them.

### A â€” Don't Hoard

No strict numerical limit, but hoarding more working vehicles than you have active group members is poor sportsmanship. Ask yourself: *"Do I actually need this, or am I just collecting it?"*

Towing wrecks back for repair projects is totally fine and encouraged.

### B â€” Leave It Drivable

You cannot strip drive-critical parts from a vehicle that still runs. A vehicle is considered **drivable** if it has:

- Engine quality at or above 40%
- Four functional tires
- A battery
- A driver's seat
- A gas tank

Don't leave a useless husk behind.

### C â€” What You Can Strip

Non-essential parts are fair game on any vehicle, even a drivable one:

> Hoods, trunk lids, doors, seats *(except driver's)*, windows, radios, light bulbs
